# Roles & Belts

## The Belt Hierarchy
Six Sigma uses a martial arts-inspired belt system to designate levels of expertise.

### White Belt
- **Role**: Understanding basic concepts.
- **Focus**: Awareness and support of local projects.

### Yellow Belt
- **Role**: Project team member.
- **Focus**: Reviewing process improvements, participating in projects.

### Green Belt
- **Role**: Project leader / Part-time.
- **Focus**: Leading smaller projects, assisting Black Belts.
- **Time**: 25-50% of time dedicated to improvements.

### Black Belt
- **Role**: Project leader / Full-time.
- **Focus**: Leading complex, cross-functional projects, mentoring Green Belts.
- **Mastery**: Advanced statistics, change management.

### Master Black Belt (MBB)
- **Role**: Trainer, Coach, Strategist.
- **Focus**: Training Black/Green Belts, strategic deployment, portfolio management.

### Champion / Sponsor
- **Role**: Executive leader.
- **Focus**: Removing barriers, allocating resources, aligning projects with business goals.
