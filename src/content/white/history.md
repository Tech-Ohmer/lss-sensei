# History of Lean & Six Sigma

## The Origins of Six Sigma
Six Sigma was developed by **Bill Smith** at **Motorola** in 1986. It was later popularized by **Jack Welch** at **General Electric (GE)** in the mid-1990s.

## The Origins of Lean
Lean manufacturing principles originated from the **Toyota Production System (TPS)**, developed by **Taiichi Ohno** and **Shigeo Shingo**.

## The Merger: Lean Six Sigma
While Six Sigma focuses on reducing **variation** (defects), Lean focuses on eliminating **waste** (muda). Together, they form a powerful methodology for improving speed, quality, and cost.

### Key Milestones
- **1986**: Motorola introduces Six Sigma.
- **1995**: Jack Welch makes Six Sigma central to GE's strategy.
- **2000s**: Integration of Lean and Six Sigma into LSS.
